Our Team

Andreia Bessa

Human Rights educator & trainer with a social psychology background, Andreia is a participatory arts practitioner, hoping for a grassroot revolution for a world based on equality and human rights for all. The constant process of developing, exploring new ways to engage with others, to grow together and rebuild a different system based on social justice are important things to her.

Marik de Koning

Marik de Koning is a trainer and teacher in the field of diversity, inclusion, and anything else that seems fun and complex. Their favourite question is “why?”. At the moment they are exploring themes like vulnerability and how to be radically honest. Also, they like to cuddle with their cat, and on a windy day you can find them on a windsurf board.

David Limaverde

PhD in Arts and Education (University of Barcelona) and a master in Theater Studies (University of Amsterdam) He has a very solid experience in physical theater, participatory/community methodologies, shiatsu and butoh – a Japanese modern dance form. Our History

It is great when you find someone that shares your views on human rights and is willing to put their time and effort in improving what they saw as injustice. This happened many years ago with Anna, Tatiana, Catia, and Andreia. Thus, we took the lead and registered Stichting CAAT Projects in January 2016. We started our operations in The Hague, together with other organizations, like the The Hague Youth Factory. Years later we moved our operational centre to Amsterdam, to the OT301 building, in a close cooperation with Home of Participation. The social actors in CAAT have changed and we now we have integrated other colleagues as Marik and Tom, as well as David, Marzieh and Joe in our board. We have also the pleasure of collaborating with an enthusiastic group of volunteers and friends. Despite the changes in location and team, our core value continues the same – human rights for all. We will keep on building an impressive record of successful projects in the Netherlands and abroad, achieving the goals set in the organizational planning. Moreover, CAAT Projects has also been delivering on the requirements of the EU funded projects and bringing people together from various backgrounds. Our door keeps on being open to new opportunities, projects and collaboration either with organizations or individuals. If you think you share similar values than us, contact us and we would brainstorm together.
